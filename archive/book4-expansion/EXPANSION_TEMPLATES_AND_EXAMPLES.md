### Template 1: Character Internal Monologue

**Use when**: Character is alone, processing events, making decisions  
**Length**: 300-800 words  
**Structure**:

```markdown
[Action/sensory detail grounding the scene]

[Internal thought responding to external stimulus]

[Memory or comparison to past experience]

[Emotional response to the memory]

[Present situation analysis from character's perspective]

[Decision or resolve forming]

[Physical action reflecting internal state]
```

**Example - Riven POV**:

```markdown
I stood at the window of my study, watching dawn light paint the gardens 
in shades of amber. The blood roses had bloomed overnight‚ÄîRaechelle's 
favorite, their crimson petals edged in black like elegant mourning.

Five years of peace. Five years of watching Elena grow into someone 
remarkable. Five years of believing I'd earned the right to keep my secrets.

And now someone knew.

The Prime Archon symbol burned in my mind‚Äîfive points radiating from a 
central circle, carved into my own wards by someone powerful enough to 
breach them and confident enough to announce their knowledge. Marcus's 
ring felt heavy on my finger, the suppression artifact that had hidden 
my nature for fifteen years suddenly seeming fragile, inadequate.

I'd always known this moment would come eventually. Power like mine 
couldn't stay hidden forever, not in a world where knowledge meant 
survival and secrets meant opportunity. But I'd hoped‚Äîfoolishly, 
perhaps‚Äîthat I'd have more time. Time to train Elena. Time to 
prepare the Council. Time to build enough trust that revelation 
wouldn't shatter everything I'd worked to create.

The coffee on my desk had gone cold hours ago, untouched. I couldn't 
remember when I'd poured it, only that the ritual of morning coffee 
had seemed important, normal, a tether to the peaceful life I was 
about to lose.

No. Not lose. Defend. There was a difference, and I needed to 
remember that.

I pressed my palm against the window glass, feeling the faint 
vibration of the wards layered throughout the estate. My blood 
magic, Raechelle's ancient power, even traces of Elena's emerging 
abilities woven into the defenses. A family's protection, decades 
in the making.

Whatever came next, we'd face it together. That had to be enough.
```

**Key Elements**:
- Sensory grounding (dawn, roses, cold coffee)
- Internal conflict (peace vs. revelation)
- Emotional vulnerability (fear, hope, resolve)
- Physical details reflecting state (cold coffee, heavy ring)
- Character voice (Riven's measured, strategic thinking)
- Forward momentum (decision to defend rather than lose)

### Template 2: Environmental Description

**Use when**: Introducing new location or re-establishing familiar one  
**Length**: 400-700 words  
**Structure**:

```markdown
[Wide shot establishing the space]

[Specific architectural/design details]

[Sensory details: sounds, smells, temperature, texture]

[How the space is currently being used]

[Historical or symbolic significance]

[Character's emotional response to the space]

[Transition to action within the space]
```

### Template 3: Dialogue Expansion

**Use when**: Existing dialogue feels too abbreviated or efficient  
**Length**: Add 200-500 words per major exchange  
**Structure**:

```markdown
[Speaker A: Opening statement]

[Physical description of how it's said]

[Speaker B: Response]

[Internal reaction from POV character]

[Speaker A: Elaboration with new information]

[Speaker C: Interjection or supporting comment]

[Environmental reaction or interruption]

[Continuation of exchange with deeper exploration]

[Resolution or transition to next topic]
```

**Example Expansion**:

**BEFORE** (minimalist dialogue):
```markdown

"They're increasing in frequency," Lucian agreed. "Traditional training 
isn't working."

"Then we need new methods," I said. "I'll present a proposal at the 
next Council meeting."
```

**AFTER** (expanded with depth):
```markdown

cutting through the post-rally celebrations with an urgency that made 
nearby conversations pause. She stood with her arms crossed, tension 
visible in the set of her shoulders despite her attempt to project calm.

expression shifting from activist passion to genuine concern. "They're

That last comment sent ice down my spine. If Lucian was right, if the

"Then we need new methods," I said, the decision crystallizing even as

"Maybe," I admitted. "But I have to try. Because if we don't fix this,

```

### Template 4: Action Scene Enhancement

**Use when**: Combat or intense physical activity feels summarized  
**Length**: Add 800-1,500 words per major action sequence  
**Structure**:

```markdown
[Setup: positioning and immediate threat]

[First exchange with specific techniques]

[Sensory overload: sights, sounds, physical sensations]

[Strategic thinking mid-combat]

[Setback or complication]

[Adaptation and counter-strategy]

[Physical toll described]

[Climactic moment with full detail]

[Immediate aftermath and consequences]
```

```markdown

pitched, discordant, fundamentally wrong in a way that bypassed rational 
hearing and hit directly on instinctual horror. I was moving before

The tendrils thrashed in desperation, a final surge of void-energy that

I twisted, the darkness grazing my arm instead of piercing my chest.

Pain exploded where it touched‚Äînot physical pain but existential agony,

lost focus, felt my working waver‚Äî

‚Äîand Zephyra covered, her power surging to maintain pressure on the breach 
while I regained my footing. Three seconds of coverage. That was all I got.

I pulled myself together through sheer stubbornness, rebuilt my working, 
and slammed back into the seal. Ten more seconds of synchronized force,

I dropped to one knee, gasping, my right arm hanging limp where the void
```

### Template 5: Relationship Development Scene

**Use when**: Characters need deeper connection or evolution shown  
**Length**: 500-900 words  
**Structure**:

```markdown
[Setup: characters in private or semi-private space]

[Physical comfort/proximity established]

[Verbal exchange starting surface-level]

[Vulnerability from one character]

[Response showing understanding]

[Deeper truth revealed]

[Physical or verbal intimacy moment]

[Resolution or anticipation of future]
```

**Example - Riven and Raechelle**:

```markdown
I found Raechelle in the eastern garden, sitting on the stone bench where 
we'd had our first real conversation seventeen years ago. The blood roses 
surrounded her, their dark petals luminous in the moonlight. She wasn't 
moving, just sitting with that supernatural stillness that vampires could 
achieve when they wanted to fade into the environment.

"I thought you'd be here," I said quietly, settling beside her without 
asking permission. After this many years together, some things didn't 
need asking.

"Predictable?" she asked, a slight smile touching her lips.

"Consistent. There's a difference." I leaned back against the cool stone, 
letting the evening's tension drain from my shoulders. "Are we going to 
talk about the Council meeting, or are we going to sit in comfortable 
silence and pretend today didn't happen?"

"Can't we do both?" Her hand found mine, fingers interlacing with the 
natural ease of a thousand repetitions. Through our bond, I felt her 
emotional state‚Äîworry, determination, and underneath it all, a thread 
of fear she'd never admit to out loud.

"You're scared," I said softly. Not an accusation, just acknowledgment.

"I'm terrified," she admitted, the honesty hitting harder because of how 
rarely she let her shields down completely. "For fifteen years, we've 
had peace. We've built a life. We've watched Elena grow into someone 
extraordinary. And now..." She gestured vaguely at the estate, at the 
wards still bearing the mark of our mystery visitor. "Now someone knows 
what you are, and everything we've built is suddenly fragile."

"Everything's always been fragile," I said. "We just got comfortable 
pretending otherwise."

"That's very philosophical for someone who's terrified too." She turned 
to face me fully, crimson eyes reflecting moonlight. "I can feel it 
through the bond, you know. You're better at projecting calm than most, 
but you can't hide from me."

No, I couldn't. That was the beauty and terror of a familiar bond‚Äî
complete emotional transparency with one person, whether you wanted it 
or not.

"I'm scared of losing you," I admitted. "Both of you‚Äîyou and Elena. 
If this goes wrong, if the Devourer is real and as powerful as Zephyra 
suggests, if the Council turns on me, if someone decides a dead Prime 
Archon is safer than a living one..." I couldn't finish the thought.

"Then we face it," Raechelle said with the certainty of centuries. "We've 
faced worse, Riven. We've faced Terravos, ancient vampires, Council 
politics, your parents' murder, my sire's machinations‚Äîwe survived all 
of it because we faced it together. This isn't different."

"This is completely different. This is cosmic-level threat meets political 
nightmare meets family in danger. It's every fear I've ever had 
simultaneously manifesting."

"I know." She shifted closer, her head coming to rest on my shoulder in 
a gesture of vulnerability she rarely showed anywhere but here, in this 
garden, with me. "But here's what you're missing in your catastrophic 
thinking: we're not the same people who faced those earlier threats. 
We're stronger now. Smarter. More connected. Elena's not a child to 
protect anymore‚Äîshe's a warrior in her own right. We have allies, 
resources, knowledge. Yes, the threat is massive. But so are we."

I wanted to argue, to list all the ways this could go wrong. Instead, 
I wrapped my arm around her shoulders and let myself draw strength from 
her certainty. She'd always been better at this than me‚Äîat finding solid 
ground when everything felt like quicksand.

"Sometimes I wonder what I did to deserve you," I said quietly.

"You were yourself," she replied simply. "Flawed, scared, determined, 
impossibly stubborn, and brave enough to let me see all of it. That's 
what I bonded with‚Äînot your power or your destiny or your role. You. 
Just you."

We sat in silence after that, but it wasn't uncomfortable. It was the 
silence of two people who'd fought together, built together, loved 
together for nearly two decades. The silence of partnership tested by 
fire and emerging stronger.

Whatever came next‚ÄîDevourer, Council politics, ancient mysteries‚Äîwe'd 
face it the same way we'd faced everything else. Together. And that had 
to be enough.
```

**Elements Included**:
- Physical setting with emotional resonance
- Comfortable intimacy established
- Vulnerability progression (surface‚Üídeep)
- Bond mechanics shown
- Individual fears acknowledged
- Strength drawn from partnership
- Physical affection appropriate to relationship
- Reassurance without dismissing concerns
- Emotional honesty
- Forward-facing resolution

## üîß EXPANSION TROUBLESHOOTING

### Problem: Addition Feels Like Padding

**Symptoms**:
- Sentences that don't reveal anything new
- Descriptions that don't enhance understanding
- Conversations that circle without progressing
- Reader would skip without losing comprehension

**Solutions**:
1. **Multi-purpose content**: Every addition must serve 2-3 functions
   - Advance plot AND reveal character
   - Build world AND develop relationship
   - Create atmosphere AND foreshadow future

2. **Cut ruthlessly**: If it doesn't earn its place, remove it
3. **Vary sentence structure**: Avoid repetitive patterns
4. **Test**: Can you remove it without loss? If yes, it's padding.

---

### Problem: Character Voice Drifts

**Symptoms**:
- Character sounds like different person
- Vocabulary shifts inappropriately
- Thought patterns don't match original
- Formality level changes

**Solutions**:
1. **Create voice card**: List character's speech patterns
   - Common phrases they use
   - Sentence structure preferences
   - Humor style
   - Internal monologue rhythm
   
2. **Read original content before adding to same character**
3. **Use character voice checklist**:
   - Does this sound like them?
   - Would they phrase it this way?
   - Is the thought process consistent?
   - Does vocabulary match education/background?

---

### Problem: Pacing Drags

**Symptoms**:
- Too much description between action beats
- Conversations that meander
- Internal monologue that loops
- Readers losing thread of plot

**Solutions**:
1. **Establish rhythm**: Action ‚Üí Reflection ‚Üí Action ‚Üí Quiet ‚Üí Intense
2. **Hook every 2-3 pages**: Question raised, tension increased, reveal delivered
3. **Trim transitional content**: Get characters where they need to be quickly
4. **Use short paragraphs**: Break up dense description with white space
5. **Vary chapter structure**: Not all chapters need same pacing

---

### Problem: Continuity Errors Creeping In

**Symptoms**:
- Character knows something they shouldn't
- Timeline doesn't add up
- Description contradicts earlier text
- Abilities appear/disappear

**Solutions**:
1. **Maintain continuity bible**: Update after every addition
2. **Cross-reference constantly**: Check earlier mentions before adding
3. **Track timeline**: Every time reference must align
4. **Flag uncertainties**: Mark for verification if unsure
5. **Use search**: Find all mentions of element before contradicting
