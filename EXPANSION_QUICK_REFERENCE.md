## üéØ EXPANSION PRIORITIES (In Order)

1. **Character Internal Life** (~600-900 words/chapter)
   - Internal monologue
   - Emotional processing
   - Decision-making thoughts
   - Memory/flashback moments

2. **World-Building Detail** (~500-800 words/chapter)
   - Environmental descriptions
   - Sensory details
   - Cultural elements
   - Daily life textures

3. **Dialogue Expansion** (~500-800 words/chapter)
   - Full conversations vs. summaries
   - Multiple speakers in group scenes
   - Physical actions during talking
   - Strategic debates fully shown

4. **Action Enhancement** (~1000-1500 words/major scene)
   - Combat tactics detailed
   - Physical sensations described
   - Strategic thinking shown
   - Consequences emphasized

5. **Subplot Development** (~500-800 words/chapter)
   - Romance progression
   - Political maneuvering
   - Personal relationships
   - Secondary character arcs

6. **Pacing Modulation** (~400-700 words/chapter)
   - Quiet reflection moments
   - Transitional breathing space
   - Character alone with thoughts
   - Routine activities shown

### Riven Sixxx (Book 4 POV)

**Voice Characteristics**:
- First-person, measured tone
- Strategic thinker - analyzes situations
- Self-deprecating humor under stress
- Formal in public, warm with family
- Protective instincts drive decisions
- Guilt and responsibility weigh heavily
- Ancient power references matter-of-fact

**Key Phrases**:

- Impulsive decisions without analysis
- Ignoring consequences
- Excessive optimism

- Idealistic but learning pragmatism

- Accepting authority without question

## ‚ö†Ô∏è COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

1. **Info-Dumping**: Don't explain everything at once
   - ‚úì Weave details naturally through scenes
   - ‚úó Paragraph-long explanations stopping action

2. **Repetitive Descriptions**: Don't reuse same details
   - ‚úì Vary how you describe recurring elements
   - ‚úó "Crimson blood magic flared" every single time

3. **Static Dialogue**: Don't just exchange information
   - ‚úì Add physical actions, reactions, interruptions
   - ‚úó "Character A said. Character B said. Character A said."

4. **Telling vs. Showing**: Don't summarize emotions
   - ‚úì "His hands trembled, blood magic flickering"
   - ‚úó "He was scared and tired"

5. **Pacing Monotony**: Don't maintain same speed throughout
   - ‚úì Vary between intense and reflective
   - ‚úó Constant high-action or constant slow description

## üé® SCENE TYPE FORMULAS

### Internal Monologue Scene
**Structure**: Action ‚Üí Thought ‚Üí Memory ‚Üí Emotion ‚Üí Resolution ‚Üí Action  
**Length**: 500-800 words  
**Purpose**: Character depth, decision-making, emotional processing

### Dialogue Scene
**Structure**: Setup ‚Üí Exchange ‚Üí Elaboration ‚Üí Complication ‚Üí Resolution  
**Length**: 600-1200 words  
**Purpose**: Relationship development, information sharing, conflict

### Action Scene
**Structure**: Setup ‚Üí First Strike ‚Üí Complication ‚Üí Adaptation ‚Üí Climax ‚Üí Aftermath  
**Length**: 1000-1500 words  
**Purpose**: Advance plot, show abilities, create tension

### World-Building Scene
**Structure**: Wide View ‚Üí Details ‚Üí Sensory ‚Üí Historical ‚Üí Character Response  
**Length**: 400-700 words  
**Purpose**: Enrich setting, establish atmosphere, ground reader

### Relationship Scene
**Structure**: Proximity ‚Üí Surface Talk ‚Üí Vulnerability ‚Üí Understanding ‚Üí Intimacy  
**Length**: 500-900 words  
**Purpose**: Deepen bonds, show character sides, emotional anchoring
