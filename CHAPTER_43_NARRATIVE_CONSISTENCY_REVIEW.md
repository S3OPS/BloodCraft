### Issue 1: Casualty Count Tracking

**Severity**: MINOR  
**Impact**: Minimal; could cause very careful readers to need to re-read for clarity  
**Category**: Narrative clarity

**The Problem**:
The casualty count has a minor presentation issue that could momentarily confuse readers tracking the math:

- Line 185: Viktor reports: *"Five dead... Two of mine, three civilians who were too close when the tear opened. Nineteen seriously wounded."*
- Line 186: *"And that's not counting the two scouts who were erased entirely at the beginning."*
- Line 188: *"Seven casualties."*

**Analysis**:
The math is technically correct: 5 dead + 2 erased = 7 total casualties. However, the presentation could be slightly clearer. The phrase "not counting the two scouts" in line 186 might make readers think those are *additional* to the five dead, when actually Viktor is clarifying that the five dead are conventional deaths while the two scouts represent a different category (erasure).

**Recommended Fix**:

Line 184-188 - Rewrite for clarity:
```markdown
OLD:
"Final count?" I asked, dreading the answer.

"Five dead," he reported grimly. "Two of mine, three civilians who were too close when the tear opened. Nineteen seriously wounded. And that's not counting the two scouts who were erased entirely at the beginning."

Seven casualties.

NEW:
"Final count?" I asked, dreading the answer.

"Seven total," he reported grimly. "Five conventional deaths‚Äîtwo of mine, three civilians who were too close when the tear opened. Plus the two scouts from the beginning who were erased entirely. Nineteen more seriously wounded."

Seven casualties. Each one a weight on my conscience‚Äîpeople who died because the universe itself was trying to correct the paradox of my existence.
```

**Alternative Fix** (Minimal change):
```markdown

OLD: "And that's not counting the two scouts who were erased entirely at the beginning."
NEW: "That includes the two scouts who were erased entirely at the beginning‚Äîseven total casualties."
```
