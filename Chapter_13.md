# Blood Craft: Chapter 13

**Book One of the Blood Craft Series**

---

## Chapter 13: The Sanctioned Duel

The summons arrived at dawn.

Not a polite request or a formal invitation, but a rolled parchment sealed with wax and delivered by a courier who dropped it on our doorstep and departed without a word. The seal bore a symbol I didn't recognize—a clenched fist surrounded by mountain peaks.

Raechelle took one look at it and her expression hardened. "Ironclad clan."

"Should I be concerned?" I broke the seal and unrolled the parchment, noting the heavy weight of the paper and the formal script.

"Depends on what it says."

I read aloud: "*Riven Sixxx, Blood Archon and newcomer to Nocturne—You are hereby challenged to formal combat under the ancient rites of the Blood Moon Accord. The challenge is issued by Viktor Ironclad, Alpha's son, for the honor of his pack and the restoration of his family's standing. You have three days to accept or decline. Refusal will be noted. The duel, if accepted, will take place in the Grand Arena at the next full moon. May the worthy prevail.*"

Silence filled the breakfast room. Through our bond, I felt Raechelle's carefully controlled fury—a white-hot rage she was working hard to contain.

"Tell me about Viktor Ironclad," I said calmly, setting the parchment aside.

"Arrogant. Ambitious. The youngest son of the Ironclad Alpha, which means he has something to prove." She paced like the predator she was, tail lashing in agitation. "He's been looking for an opportunity to make a name for himself ever since his older brother was chosen as heir apparent."

"And challenging me accomplishes that how?"

"You're a Blood Archon, Sir. Ancient lineage, considerable power, and relatively inexperienced in Nocturne's politics." Her amber eyes met mine. "Defeating you in sanctioned combat would establish him as a serious contender for leadership positions. It would prove the werewolves are still relevant in Nocturne's power structure."

I considered this. "And if I decline?"

"Your reputation suffers. You'll be seen as weak, afraid. Future challenges will multiply." She stopped pacing, her expression shifting from anger to calculation. "This was probably coordinated with the framing attempt. Isolate you politically, then force you into a corner where any choice damages your standing."

"Terravos," I growled.

"Almost certainly. Viktor wouldn't have the subtlety to plan this alone, but he'd eagerly serve as a tool for someone like the Earth Archon's ambassador." Raechelle moved to my side, her hand finding mine. "But we can use this, Sir. A public victory over a werewolf champion would solidify your position in ways quiet maneuvering never could."

"You think I can beat him?"

"I know you can." Her confidence flooded through our bond, warm and absolute. "Viktor is strong, fast, and experienced in combat. But you're a Blood Archon coming into your full power. You have advantages he can't imagine."

I looked at the parchment again, noting the deadline. "Three days to prepare. Then the full moon—that's what, a week after that?"

"Ten days total." Raechelle squeezed my hand. "Enough time to train properly. To learn what we're facing. To turn this ambush into an opportunity."

The decision crystallized with surprising ease. I was tired of reacting, of letting enemies dictate the terms of engagement. Viktor Ironclad wanted a duel? Fine. I'd give him one he'd never forget.

"I accept," I said. "Draft a formal response. Make it clear that I'm honored by the challenge and look forward to the confrontation."

Raechelle's smile was savage. "Now you're thinking like an Archon."

---

The next three days passed in a blur of intense preparation.

Master Blackwood, when informed of the duel, had shown the closest thing to approval I'd seen from him. "Good," he'd growled. "You were getting too comfortable in the classroom. Combat is a Blood Archon's truest test."

Our training intensified immediately. Where before I'd been learning defensive wards and basic combat theory, now Blackwood drilled me relentlessly in practical applications. How to sense an opponent's movement before they struck. How to use blood magic not as a weapon but as an extension of my awareness. How to turn an enemy's strength against them.

"Werewolves are faster than you," he explained during one brutal session. "Stronger in raw physical power. Viktor in particular is known for overwhelming opponents with sheer ferocity."

"So I lose in a direct confrontation."

"In a purely physical one, yes. But you're not purely physical, are you, boy?" Blackwood's eyes glittered. "You're a Blood Archon. The blood in Viktor's veins sings to you. His enhanced metabolism means his heart beats faster, pumps harder. Every advantage he has becomes a vulnerability you can exploit."

The concept was elegant in its simplicity. I spent hours practicing blood-sensing techniques, learning to feel the pulse of life in everything around me. Not just supernatural beings, but the rats in the walls, the birds in the eternal twilight sky, the very worms in the earth beneath Nocturne.

And once I could sense blood, I could influence it.

Not control—that required far more power than I currently possessed. But influence. Slow a heartbeat by a fraction. Increase blood pressure just enough to cause momentary dizziness. Create phantom pains that distracted without truly harming.

"You're not trying to kill him," Blackwood reminded me. "Sanctioned duels end when one combatant yields or is rendered unable to continue. Murder is forbidden. Break that rule and the Council will have your head, Blood Archon or not."

"I understand."

"Do you?" The old vampire studied me with unsettling intensity. "Because in the heat of battle, with blood singing in your veins and power begging to be unleashed, understanding becomes difficult. You'll want to destroy him. To prove your superiority absolutely. That impulse is your greatest danger."

I met his gaze steadily. "I'm not my power. It serves me, not the other way around."

"Pretty words. Let's see if they hold true when Viktor is trying to tear your throat out."

---

Raechelle's training was different but no less crucial. She drilled me on werewolf physiology, pack tactics, the cultural expectations surrounding formal combat. Werewolves valued honor and strength above all—showing either weakness or excessive cruelty would work against me with the spectators.

"You need to win decisively but honorably," she explained. "Demonstrate skill, not savagery. Respect Viktor's courage even as you defeat him."

"That's a narrow line to walk."

"You're a Blood Archon about to duel a werewolf in front of most of Nocturne's power structure." Her expression was wry. "Nothing about this will be easy."

We also worked on our bond, strengthening the connection that allowed us to share senses and emotions. If Viktor somehow managed to blind me or otherwise compromise my awareness, Raechelle's sight would become my own. If I was injured, her presence through the bond would help manage the pain.

"I don't like this," she admitted on the night before the full moon. We were in bed, tangled together in the quiet darkness. "Intellectually, I know you can win. But the thought of watching you fight, potentially hurt, while I can do nothing..."

"You'll be doing everything," I assured her, holding her closer. "You'll be my anchor. My connection to what matters beyond the fight itself."

"Promise me you'll be careful."

"I promise I'll do whatever it takes to come back to you." I kissed her forehead. "Viktor is strong, but I'm stronger. Not because of my power, but because I have you."

She was quiet for a long moment, and I felt complex emotions swirling through our bond—fear, pride, love, and something deeper. Something primal that she was holding back.

"What is it, Kitten?"

"After the duel," she whispered. "Win or lose, survive or fall... you should know that I've never bonded with anyone the way I've bonded with you. You're not just my partner or my Master. You're my everything."

The intensity of her words stole my breath. "Raechelle—"

"I know we haven't discussed the future in concrete terms. Marriage, children, forever." She looked up at me, amber eyes luminous in the darkness. "But I need you to understand. There is no future for me that doesn't include you. If you fall tomorrow—"

"I won't."

"But if you do," she insisted, "I fall with you. That's what the bond means. That's what you mean to me."

I held her gaze, letting her see the truth in my own eyes. "Then I guess I'll have to win. Because I'm not ready to let you go either. Not now, not ever."

We made love then, slow and intense, reaffirming our connection in the most fundamental way. Every touch carried meaning. Every kiss was a promise. By the time we finally slept, wrapped around each other in the darkness, I felt more centered than I had since my parents' death.

Tomorrow I would face Viktor Ironclad in front of hundreds of supernatural beings, many of whom wanted to see me fail.

But tonight, I had this. I had her. I had us.

It would be enough. It had to be.

---

The Grand Arena occupied a massive cavern beneath Nocturne's central district—a natural amphitheater that had been enhanced over centuries with magical architecture and defensive wards. Tiers of stone seating rose in concentric circles, enough to accommodate thousands of spectators. The floor itself was smooth obsidian, inscribed with intricate runes that would contain the combat and protect the audience from stray magic.

I arrived with Raechelle an hour before the scheduled duel, as tradition demanded. She wore formal attire—a elegant dress in deep crimson that matched her hair—while I was dressed in simple combat leathers that Master Blackwood had procured. "Nothing fancy," he'd advised. "You're not here to look pretty. You're here to fight."

The arena was already half full. Vampires occupied the upper tiers, their pale faces luminous in the magical lighting. Werewolves claimed the lower sections closest to the action, their energy crackling through the space. Witches and other supernatural beings filled the middle sections, a diverse crowd united by curiosity about the newcomer Blood Archon.

Lady Seraphina Darkthorne presided from a private box at the arena's north end, accompanied by several Council members. She caught my eye as I entered and offered the slightest nod—acknowledgment but not favoritism. The Council would observe impartially, as required by the ancient rites.

Viktor Ironclad stood at the far end of the arena floor. He was exactly what I'd expected—massive, muscular, with the predatory grace unique to werewolves in their human form. His dark hair was pulled back in a warrior's braid, his chest bare to display the ritual scars that marked his rank within the pack. When he saw me, his lip curled in something between a sneer and a smile.

"Blood Archon," he called, his voice carrying easily across the space. "I wasn't sure you'd come."

"I gave my word." I walked forward until only twenty feet separated us. "I always keep my word."

"Noble." He circled slowly, assessing. "We'll see how noble you remain when I'm tearing your throat out."

"Bold words." I matched his circling, noting the way he moved, the distribution of his weight. "Let's see if you can back them up."

A figure emerged from the shadows near the Council box—an ancient werewolf with silver hair and eyes like winter ice. The arena fell silent at his appearance.

"Elder Marcus," Raechelle whispered beside me. "The pack's law-speaker. He'll oversee the duel."

Elder Marcus raised his hands, and magical silence fell over the crowd. "We gather under the ancient rites of the Blood Moon Accord. Two warriors stand before us, each representing their power and honor. Viktor Ironclad of the Mountain Pack, and Riven Sixxx, Blood Archon heir. The challenge has been issued and accepted. The terms are clear—combat continues until one combatant yields or is rendered unable to fight. Lethal force is forbidden. Honor above all."

He looked at each of us in turn. "Do you both understand and accept these terms?"

"I do," Viktor growled.

"I do," I confirmed.

"Then by the power vested in me by the Council of Nocturne and the traditions of our people..." Elder Marcus's voice rose to a commanding boom. "Begin!"

Viktor moved with shocking speed, closing the distance between us in a heartbeat. His first strike was a straight punch aimed at my face—testing my reactions, my defenses. I slipped left, feeling the wind of his fist pass my ear, and countered with a precise strike to his ribs.

My knuckles met solid muscle. It was like hitting a stone wall.

Viktor's laugh was savage. "That's it? That's the vaunted power of the Blood Archon?"

He pressed his attack, a flurry of punches and kicks that forced me backward. Each strike was powerful enough to shatter bone if it landed cleanly. I blocked what I could, dodged the rest, but I could already feel the difference in our physical capabilities. Viktor was right—in a purely physical contest, he was superior.

Good thing I wasn't fighting purely physically.

I opened my senses the way Blackwood had taught me, feeling for the life force that pulsed through Viktor's enhanced form. His heart was a drum of power, beating twice as fast as a human's. His blood sang with supernatural vitality, rushing through veins and arteries with tremendous force.

And now that I could feel it, I could touch it.

I dodged another combination, then reached out with my power—not dramatically, not with obvious magic, just a subtle influence on the blood flowing to Viktor's left leg. I slowed it, just a fraction. Not enough to cripple, just enough to create the tiniest delay in his movements.

His next kick came a split-second slower than he'd intended. I was already moving, stepping inside his guard and landing a solid strike to his solar plexus. This time, my fist sank in. Not much, but enough.

Viktor's eyes widened with surprise. "What—"

I didn't let him finish. I pressed my advantage, mixing physical strikes with subtle blood manipulation. Slow his right arm. Speed up the blood flow to his head, creating momentary disorientation. Phantom pains in his joints that made him hesitate.

None of it was enough to stop him. But combined with my improving physical technique, it was enough to even the playing field.

The crowd was roaring now, sensing the shift in momentum. Viktor's initial confidence was eroding, replaced by frustration as his seemingly certain victory slipped away.

"Fight me properly!" he snarled, abandoning technique for raw aggression. "Stop hiding behind tricks!"

"This is how Blood Archons fight." I caught his arm mid-swing and twisted, using his own momentum against him. "We don't need to be the strongest when we can be the smartest."

I threw him—actually threw him—sending his massive form tumbling across the obsidian floor. The arena erupted in shocked exclamations. A Blood Archon, newly awakened and barely trained, had just manhandled a werewolf champion.

Viktor rose slowly, blood trickling from a split lip. His eyes had gone wolf-gold, the beast inside straining against his control. "You want to see real power?" His voice was deeper now, rougher. "I'll show you power."

The transformation began.

"Sir!" Raechelle's mental voice cut through my concentration. "He's not supposed to shift! It's against the duel rules!"

But Viktor was beyond rules now. His body expanded, muscles bulging grotesquely as fur erupted across his skin. Bones cracked and reformed. His face elongated into a muzzle filled with gleaming fangs. In seconds, where a man had stood, a massive werewolf crouched—nine feet of killing power, all pretense of honor forgotten.

The crowd's reaction was instant. Werewolves shouted in dismay. Vampires called for intervention. The Council members leaned forward in their box, conferring urgently.

Elder Marcus's voice boomed across the arena: "Viktor Ironclad! You violate the sacred terms! Resume your human form or face—"

But Viktor wasn't listening. With a howl that shook the arena's foundations, he launched himself at me.

Time seemed to slow. I could see every detail—the saliva dripping from his fangs, the murderous rage in his golden eyes, the powerful muscles bunching for a killing strike. He was beautiful and terrible, and he was going to tear me apart.

Unless I stopped him.

My power rose like a crimson tide. Not subtle anymore, not carefully controlled. This was the full force of my Blood Archon heritage, awakened by genuine danger and shaped by weeks of training. I reached out with everything I had and seized Viktor's blood—all of it, simultaneously.

His attack faltered mid-leap. His body went rigid, caught in mid-air by my power. I could feel his heart straining against my influence, his blood screaming to flow naturally. It would be so easy to stop it. To squeeze until his heart burst. To prove my superiority absolutely.

*That impulse is your greatest danger.*

Blackwood's warning echoed in my mind. I was standing at a precipice. One choice would make me a killer, a monster who used his power without restraint. The other would make me something better.

I chose.

Instead of crushing Viktor's heart, I did something far more challenging. I slowed it. Gently, precisely, I used my power to calm the supernatural metabolism that fueled his rage. I whispered to his blood, not commanding but requesting. I reminded his body what it felt like to be human.

Viktor crashed to the floor, the transformation reversing as quickly as it had begun. In seconds he was human again, gasping and shaking, sweat pouring from his skin.

I released my hold on his blood and stepped back, giving him space.

The arena was silent. Thousands of supernatural beings watched, waiting to see what I would do next.

I extended my hand to Viktor.

"The duel is over," I said clearly, my voice carrying across the space. "You fought with honor until the end. The beast took you—it happens to all warriors eventually. But you're stronger than your rage, Viktor Ironclad. Prove it. Stand."

For a long moment, Viktor stared at my hand as if he couldn't comprehend what he was seeing. Then, slowly, he reached up and grasped it.

I pulled him to his feet.

The crowd erupted. Werewolves howled approval. Vampires applauded with elegant restraint. The chaos of sound washed over us as Viktor and I stood face to face.

"You should have killed me," he said quietly. "You had the right. The power. I broke the rules, endangered you—"

"And you'll answer for that to your pack and the Council." I met his eyes steadily. "But you're not my enemy, Viktor. You were used, manipulated into this duel by people who wanted us both weakened. I won't give them the satisfaction of seeing you dead."

Understanding dawned in his expression. "The Earth Archon."

"Almost certainly. His ambassador has been very interested in my activities." I released Viktor's hand and turned to address Elder Marcus. "The duel is concluded. I claim victory but request mercy for my opponent. His honor remains intact."

Elder Marcus studied us both for a long moment. Then he nodded slowly. "So witnessed. Viktor Ironclad, you will submit to pack justice for your violation of sacred law. Riven Sixxx, Blood Archon, your victory is recognized and your mercy is noted."

He raised his voice to address the crowd. "Let all present remember this day. Remember how true power is tempered by wisdom. Remember that strength without honor is meaningless. This duel is concluded!"

The crowd's roar redoubled. I turned away from Viktor, seeking Raechelle in the chaos. She was pushing through the crowd, tears streaming down her face, her smile brilliant and beautiful.

I caught her in my arms as she reached me, lifting her off her feet and holding her close. Through our bond, I felt her overwhelming relief, her fierce pride, her absolute love.

"You did it," she whispered. "You were magnificent."

"I had good motivation." I kissed her, not caring who saw, not caring about the hundreds of eyes on us. "I promised I'd come back to you."

"And you kept that promise." She pulled back slightly, amber eyes shining. "But Sir? Next time someone challenges you to a duel? Maybe we discuss it first."

I laughed, the tension of the past days finally breaking. "Deal."

---

The celebration that followed was unlike anything I'd experienced. Word of the duel spread through Nocturne like wildfire, and by the time Raechelle and I returned to our townhouse, we found it surrounded by well-wishers. Gifts appeared on our doorstep—wines from vampire vintages centuries old, rare magical texts from grateful scholars, even a formal apology letter from the Ironclad Alpha promising an investigation into who had manipulated his son.

Lady Seraphina sent a private message: *Well done. Your restraint has won you more allies than raw victory ever could. We'll discuss this at our next meeting.*

But the most meaningful response came from Viktor himself. He arrived at our door after midnight, accompanied by two of his packmates. He looked exhausted and bruised, but his eyes were clear.

"May I speak with you?" he asked formally.

"Of course." I gestured him inside. Raechelle disappeared discreetly into the kitchen, leaving us alone in the sitting room.

Viktor didn't sit. He stood uncomfortably, clearly struggling with what he wanted to say. Finally, the words came in a rush.

"I was wrong. About you, about the duel, about everything. I let my ambition and my pride blind me to the truth—that I was being used as a weapon against someone who could have been an ally." He met my eyes. "You spared me when you had every right to end me. That mercy is a debt I can never fully repay. But I swear to you now, on my pack's honor and my own blood: I am your ally. Anyone who moves against you moves against me."

The offer was significant. A werewolf champion's protection was not given lightly.

"I accept your oath," I said formally. "And I offer you my own in return. The Blood Archon line stands with the Ironclad pack. We face our enemies together."

Relief washed over Viktor's face. He clasped my arm in the werewolf style, a warrior's greeting. "Together," he agreed.

After he left, Raechelle emerged from the kitchen with a knowing smile. "You're building an empire, Sir. One ally at a time."

"I'm building a life," I corrected. "One choice at a time."

"And what do you choose now?"

I looked at her—my familiar, my partner, my love—and the answer was obvious.

"I choose us. I choose this moment. I choose to celebrate surviving another day in this impossible city." I moved toward her, seeing my own desire reflected in her eyes. "The enemies can wait. The politics can wait. Right now, the only thing that matters is you."

"Smooth, Sir." But her smile was pure joy as I swept her into my arms. "Very smooth."

I carried her toward the bedroom, feeling lighter than I had in weeks. Yes, challenges remained. Yes, Terravos was still hunting me. Yes, mysteries and dangers lurked in every shadow.

But I had also proven something important today. I was strong enough to win. Wise enough to show mercy. Confident enough to build alliances instead of burning bridges.

I was becoming the Blood Archon my parents had always believed I could be.

And with Raechelle at my side, there was nothing I couldn't face.

The duel was over.

But my story—our story—was just beginning.
