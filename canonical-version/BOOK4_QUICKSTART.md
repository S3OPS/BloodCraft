## üí° Development Tips

### When Planning
1. Start with the ending - what's the resolution?
2. Work backward to figure out how to get there
3. Ensure each chapter advances the story
4. Plant seeds for future chapters
5. Create emotional arcs alongside plot arcs

### When Writing
1. Stay in your POV character's voice
2. Show, don't tell (when appropriate)
3. Use the magic system creatively
4. Create sensory experiences
5. Make every scene count

### When Stuck
1. Review source material for inspiration
2. Revisit your outline and themes
3. Write a different chapter and come back
4. Take a break and return fresh
5. Remember: quality over speed

## üéØ Success Metrics

### You'll Know You're On Track When:
- Story feels like a natural continuation
- Characters sound like themselves
- Plot builds on Books 1-3 without rehashing
- Each chapter advances the story
- You're excited about what you're writing
- Readers (if any) are engaged

### Warning Signs:
- Forcing plot that doesn't fit characters
- Contradicting established canon
- Losing character voices
- Repeating Books 1-3 storylines
- Writing feels like a chore

If you see warning signs, pause and reassess your direction.
