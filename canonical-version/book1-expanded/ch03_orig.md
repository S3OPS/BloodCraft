# **Chapter 3**

As we glided down the lengthy driveway of my home, a gentle jolt of realization roused me from my slumber. The world
outside the window blurred past in a haze of greens and browns, but my mind struggled to piece together how I had ended
up here. The muffled sound of the gravel crunching under the tires stirred a vague familiarity within me, yet I couldn't
recall the moments leading up to this point. It was as if I had been adrift in a dream—a nightmare—only now awakening to
the sight of the towering trees lining the path and the glow of moonlight filtering through the branches. For a precious
moment, I forgot about the accident, forgot about my parents, forgot about the blood and fire. Then reality crashed
back, and I had to fight the urge to scream.

The house is a serene retreat, perched in the midst of rolling countryside, far removed from the chaos of civilization,
deep within the charm of Central Texas, just south of Dallas. The property encompasses about 40 acres, with an inviting
driveway that stretches almost a mile, lined with wildflowers that sway gently in the breeze. The rustic two-story house
stands proud atop a gentle hill, offering sweeping views of the expansive landscape; from beneath the wraparound porch,
one can spot any visitor long before they arrive.

The structure, lovingly maintained over the years, exudes a sense of history and warmth. Its roof, adorned with
sun-bleached shingles, tells tales of countless sunny days, while the wood siding, now faded to a soft pastel yellow,
reflects two decades of stories etched into its grain. Surrounding the home, the immediate terrain unfolds as a vast,
flat expanse of lush grassland, dotted with a few resilient shrubs and stately trees that frame the property
beautifully.  Beyond the manicured yard, the land transforms dramatically into a wild and enchanting wilderness. The
remaining 35 acres are a treasure trove of dense woods, alive with the sounds of chirping birds and rustling leaves. A
small, glistening tributary meanders through the heart of the forest, its clear waters whispering as they weave their
way through, inviting exploration and adventure.

“How long have I been asleep?” I asked, rubbing the sleep from my eyes as I turned to face her. A mixture of confusion
and curiosity flickered across my features as I continued, “How did you know where I live?” My brows furrowed with
questions, searching her expression for answers while I tried to come to terms with the situation. The dim light in the
car cast shadows on her face, amplifying the intensity of the moment.

"Riven, please—let's hold off on questions until we're inside your home. You've been out cold for hours since the
accident, and I want to ensure you are settled before delving into the swarm of inquiries rattling in your mind." She
paused, her nostrils flaring slightly, and something flickered across her features—hunger? recognition? desire?—before
she quickly composed herself. "It's quite a challenge to concentrate on anything with the scent you exude; it's as if
every sense is heightened by your presence. Your awakening... it's changed you in ways that are already affecting
everything around you."

Raechelle emphasized with purpose, but I couldn't help but notice it seemed like she was struggling to breathe as she
continued.

“Trust me when I say this: I’ve unraveled secrets about your family that you may not even know. Know that I am here,
unwavering in my commitment to guide you through this journey. There’s a deep bond between us, entwined with your
bloodline, which now, heart-wrenchingly, rests solely in your hands.”

I felt a sharp stab to my pride at the mention of my odor, prompting me to take a moment to thoroughly assess myself. I
raised my arms slightly and inhaled deeply, trying to detect any hint of body odor or an unpleasant scent that could
possibly justify such a harsh comment. To my surprise, I found nothing that seemed remotely offensive.

Turning to her, I spoke with an unwavering tone, “Can you please clarify what it is about my smell that's bothering you
so much? I genuinely would like to understand. I apologize if my natural scent offends you, but honestly, I can't smell
anything that could be considered disgusting or revolting.”

“YOU DO NOT SMELL DISGUSTING OR REVOLTING, Sir,” Raechelle exclaimed, her voice bursting forth with a fervor she hadn’t
intended. The words hung in the air, a mixture of urgency and honesty that momentarily startled her. Taking a breath to
collect herself, she felt a wave of embarrassment wash over her. “Uggghhh,” she groaned, her brow furrowing slightly
with frustration, “I would venture to assume you have no control over it, but I truly didn’t anticipate just how deeply
it would impact me in the limited space of my car.”

She paused, the weight of her words sinking in. “Once you get to know me better, you might grasp why my sense of smell
is far more sensitive than most,” she explained, her tone softening as she attempted to clarify. With an impatient
glance at the building ahead, she added, “Now, please, we’re finally here; let’s just go inside.” The urgency in her
voice conveyed a blend of relief and eagerness to escape the tension that lingered in the air around them.

We left the car behind, the crunch of gravel fading into the background as we approached the looming figure of the
house. It stood there, silent and foreboding, under the shadow of the looming trees that surrounded it—the same house
that had been filled with laughter and warmth just days ago. Just days. How could everything change so completely in just
days?

The moment I crossed the threshold, a chill swept over me, an overwhelming sense of dread washing over my senses like a
cold wave. The silence hit first—not the comfortable quiet of a sleeping house, but the hollow, pressing silence of
abandonment. No hum of Dad's computer from the office. No distant sound of Mom humming while she cooked. Just... nothing.

Everything looked exactly as it had when we'd left for the trip. Mom's coffee mug still sat in the sink, a ring of dried
coffee at the bottom, lipstick marks on the rim—evidence of her last morning here. Dad's slippers rested by his favorite
chair, one slightly askew as if he'd just stepped out of them. The throw blanket Mom always draped over her lap during
movie nights was still rumpled on the couch, holding the impression of her body.

These ordinary objects—things I'd seen a thousand times without really noticing—now felt sacred and terrible all at once.
Each one a small monument to lives that would never return. I wanted to preserve them exactly as they were, like a museum
to my parents, and simultaneously I wanted to destroy everything, to not have to face these reminders of what I'd lost.

It struck me with a chilling, inescapable clarity that everything inside—the creaking floors that knew our footsteps, the
faded wallpaper Dad had always meant to replace, the lingering scent of Mom's lavender soap and Dad's coffee—now belonged
to me alone. The house itself seemed to know. It felt different, emptier, as if their absence had physically changed the
space. In that instant, I felt the weight of loneliness settle heavily upon my shoulders, a burden I would carry forever.
They were never coming back. I would never hear Mom call me for dinner. Never debate movies with Dad. Never feel complete
again.

My legs trembled, and I had to brace myself against the doorframe. How was I supposed to just... keep going? How did
people survive this?
Determined to piece together the fragments of recent events that had left me disoriented and anxious, I shifted my focus
to the task at hand: finding the journal. The urgency of my quest propelled me into action, and I began my search in the
most obvious place—the office.  I pulled open the heavy wooden door, the hinges creaking in protest, and began to
meticulously comb through my mother's bookshelves. Titles from classic literature to dusty old encyclopedias lined the
walls, but despite my thorough examination, the journal remained elusive.  After extensive searching yielded no results,
I decided to venture into the master bedroom, where my heart quickened at the thought of uncovering more memories. The
air felt thick with nostalgia as I entered, the room untouched and draped in shadows. I rifled through her dressers,
each pull of the drawer filled with the scent of lavender and old fabric, my fingers brushing against forgotten garments
and trinkets. I meticulously explored every nook and cranny, hoping to stumble upon the journal—an essential key to
understanding the enigma that loomed larger with every passing moment.

After an hour of meticulously combing through the cluttered shelves and boxes in the dimly lit attic, I turned to
Raechelle with a sense of urgency. “Do you have any idea where the journal might be?” I asked, my voice laced with
frustration.

She tilted her head slightly, her lips pinching in thought. “What did your mother say about it?” Raechelle inquired, a
hint of curiosity in her tone.

Letting out a small sigh, I ran my fingers through my hair, trying to quell the growing frustration. “Nothing much,
really,” I replied, the exasperation creeping into my voice. “She just told me to trust the signs and listen to my
heart. But what does that even mean? I just want to find the journal!”

As I relayed the troublesome message my mother had shared with me, my gaze was inadvertently drawn to an intriguing
symbol etched onto the nightstand beside me. My breath caught in my throat—it was unmistakably the same ominous emblem
that had adorned the vial from which I had sipped just hours earlier. The sight of it here, in my parents' room, sent a
shiver down my spine. How long had they kept this secret from me? Compelled by curiosity and a desperate need for
answers, I stepped closer to the nightstand and traced the contours of the symbol with my fingertips, feeling the edges
of the design under my skin. The wood seemed to pulse with a faint warmth beneath my touch. Upon closer inspection, I
noticed that the center of the pentagram appeared slightly different from what I had first observed—raised, like a
button waiting to be pressed. A whisper of instinct urged me to press down on this unusual feature, so I complied. To my
surprise, a resounding click echoed in the otherwise quiet room, signaling a mechanism at work. The past I thought I
knew was about to shatter completely. With a gentle lift, the top of the nightstand rose ever so slightly, with a mix of
excitement and trepidation, I grasped the edge of the nightstand and lifted it, revealing a compartment that had long
remained concealed. In its depths lay a heavily worn leather-bound journal, its surface marred by years of use and
whispers of forgotten stories. What caught my attention immediately was the pentagram embossed on the cover distinct
from the one I had seen earlier, boasting five separate symbols, each intricately placed at the tips of the star's five
points.

Starting from the apex, the first symbol resembled a circle, cradling a small double helix in its center—a potential
marker of something biological, a hint of life intertwined within its design.  Moving in a clockwise direction, I noted
the second symbol: a stark upside-down triangle, sharply defined and commanding attention. Next, a right-side-up
triangle sat adjacent, creating an intriguing balance between the two. The bottom left symbol was another upside-down
triangle, this one distinguished by a horizontal line bisecting it, suggesting a deeper meaning or separation. Finally,
at the leftmost point of the star, a right-side-up triangle replicated the previous design but was likewise adorned with
a horizontal line, adding layers to the symbology woven throughout this mysterious tome. Each symbol felt heavy with
meaning as if holding secrets waiting to be revealed.

As I lifted the journal, my fingers continued to trace the intricate lines of the detailed pentagram etched into its
cover. The leather was soft with age, warm to the touch, as if the book itself held life within its pages. Nestled
beneath it, a thick envelope caught my eye, its texture sturdy and inviting. My heart raced as I saw a single word
written in my mother's elegant handwriting: Riven. The mere sight of her familiar script sent a wave of nostalgia
through me, stirring memories that felt both distant and immediate—her hand guiding mine as she taught me to write, the
notes she'd leave in my lunchbox, the birthday cards filled with her love. I had to blink back tears before I could
continue.

As I reached for the letter resting in the hidden compartment, I felt my grip falter, nearly letting the journal slip
from my hands.  To steady myself, I sank down onto the edge of the bed.  With a deep breath, I placed the journal—a
weathered tome filled with my thoughts and secrets—gently beside me.  As I focused on the letter, I noticed the
intricate wax seal that adorned its flap, marked with the same pentagram I was becoming familiar with.  I took a moment
to appreciate the artistry of the seal before I carefully pulled the letter free, the crack of the wax breaking echoing
softly in the quiet room.

*Dear Riven, *

*If this letter has found its way to you, it signifies a turning point—either the unfolding of events that I must now
share or perhaps the troubling arrival of those who seek the knowledge your father and I possess. Please remember,
within your heart, that you are deeply cherished, and it pains us to have placed this heavy burden upon your young
shoulders. Our desire for a semblance of normalcy in our lives with you was driven by love, and for this fleeting
moment of selfishness, we genuinely apologize.*

*As you read these words, I’m certain you’ve taken the blood vial, the key to unlocking the dormant magic within you,
and I trust you are beginning to feel the empowering rush of your newfound abilities. In time, you will discover just

*The power you will uncover is boundless and vast; however, to achieve greatness as a mage, it is essential to master
the fundamental concepts that underlie the art.  At the core of magical practice lies the sacred pentagram, composed
of the five prime magical disciplines: Blood, Water, Fire, Earth, and Air. Each of these elements represents a
distinct pathway of magical expression, rich with its own intricacies and potential.*

*Most practitioners in the realm of magic, whether mages or sorcerers, tend to dedicate themselves to a single

navigate these challenges carefully to fully grasp the implications of your heritage.*

*The other four magical disciplines are equally potent in their own way, with each Archon honing unique abilities tied
intricately to their craft. The Archons—representatives of their elements—form a council of magical Archons,

duel and defeat them in combat.*

*In the last three hundred years, the members of the Archonis has become stagnant, with only four of five Archons
currently serving and those four have held their positions each for a century or more.  A horrific event in history
marked the end of the last mage, whose insatiable thirst for power posed a grave threat to both the magical
and mundane worlds. Their excessive manipulation of Blood Magic led to a catastrophic decision by the other Archons,
resulting in his demise. In the aftermath, fear reigned supreme, prompting a relentless hunt for anyone linked to the

hiding, leading your family to adopt a life off the grid, distanced from the broader magical community and your
destined path.*

*Reflecting on the annals of history, there was once a figure of incredible prowess known as the Prime Archon—an
enigmatic, nearly mythical being believed to possess mastery over all five disciplines, capable of manipulating time
and space. Tragically, the records detailing his existence and extraordinary feats have been largely lost or
annihilated over the centuries, particularly during the brutal purges targeting your bloodline and others like it.
Whispers and rumors suggest that the Blood Archon lineage may have traceable roots linked to this Prime Archon,
further deepening the mystique surrounding your heritage.*

*Indeed, fear is a potent weapon; it has the power to warp minds and compel individuals to commit unspeakable acts in
its name. The history of your lineage is fraught with trepidation and cautious hope, placing the weight of the world
on your shoulders as you embark on this journey of self-discovery and magical awakening.*

*I implore you to place your trust in Raechelle. She is not just a helper; she is an integral part of our family’s
tapestry, woven into our lives for ages. Bound to you as a familiar, she is a magical creature with deep connections
to our bloodline. Familiars like Rae are enchanting beings, often taking on the characteristics of various animals,
their forms and abilities reflecting the unique magic they serve. While many share traits of wolves, birds, or even
reptiles, Rae is a beautiful cat familiar, gracefully poised with her piercing eyes and sleek fur. Her loyalty knows
no bounds, and her devotion to you surpasses that of any creature you’ll ever encounter.  Lean on her wisdom, and
allow her to guide you on this extraordinary journey that awaits. You are never alone in this—Rae is here to stand by
your side, ready to help you navigate the magic and challenges ahead.*

*Something else you should know **about** Rae if you **haven't** discovered it already is, due to a rather unfortunate
and complicated series of events that unfolded many years ago, she has become a child of blood—a vampire, to put it
plainly. However, I encourage you to set aside any preconceived notions or stereotypes you might have about vampires
based on the countless movies and shows we've watched together over the years. She will educate you about her unique
world and the intricacies of her kind. She has a wealth of knowledge to share, not only about vampires but also about
other supernatural beings that inhabit her universe. If you open your mind and allow her to guide you, you’ll discover
insights and truths about her existence that go far beyond the myths and legends of popular culture.*

*It is absolutely crucial for you to understand that Rae is bound to you as your familiar. This bond is not merely a
passing connection; it is a profound and eternal link. To illustrate this, think of her as your Mary Jane, your
Gamora, your Juliet, your Belle, or your Rapunzel—essentially, she represents your everything. I can’t help but
chuckle at how much I know you will enjoy these movie references, as they encapsulate the essence of our shared
experiences.*

*However, I suspect she isn't yet aware of the depth of the bond you share. At this moment, she may feel a compelling
attraction toward you, but she won't truly understand why this is happening. It will likely confuse and frustrate her
immensely, as everything you do or say will ignite a fire within her, an urge she might instinctively want to resist.
Though she may not have conscious memories of the event from almost 15 years ago, a significant moment occurred when
you first crossed paths when you were a child—at that moment, a blood bond was forged between you.  The blood bond is
an extraordinary and powerful connection that exists between two magical beings, creating an attraction that is far
stronger than physical gravity. It’s important to remember that you do not get to choose who you become bonded with;
the intricacies of magic and its energies are as enigmatic as the bond itself. This connection transcends the limits

*Once you both recognize and accept this connection, it will establish itself in a way that makes the two of you
inseparable. When your father and I first came to understand this bond, we took decisive action to protect you

*In essence, your journey together is one of profound discovery, and as the bond unfolds, things will become clearer.
You will navigate this magical connection, and as you do, you may find that the strength of your shared bond is unlike
anything you have ever experienced.*

*I understand that this may be overwhelming for you, my dear son, but I have complete faith that you will rise to

*To assist you in this transformative process, I have left behind a comprehensive journal that your father and I
meticulously compiled over the years. This journal is filled with invaluable insights about the other Archons, their
experiences, and wisdom gleaned from their journeys. Within its pages, you will also find extensive training material
and knowledge specifically designed to help you hone your magical abilities. My greatest hope is that this journal,
coupled with the guidance of Rae, will provide you with the foundation you need until you are ready to confront the

*Moreover, nestled alongside this letter and the journal is a special ring, crafted with love and purpose by your
father. This ring has the unique power to conceal your abilities from those who might seek to uncover them. It is

*Mom*

I read the letter several times, each pass revealing layers of meaning and emotion that tugged at my heart. 

The first time through, I could barely process the words—Blood Archon, familiar bonds, protection spells. It was too much,
too fast, too impossible. My eyes skimmed over sentences without absorbing them, seeking only the shape of Mom's
handwriting, proof that she had held this paper, that her hand had moved across it with love and intention.

The second time, reality began to settle in with crushing weight. This was real. Magic was real. The supernatural world
was real. And I was supposed to be some kind of powerful being called a Blood Archon. The absurdity of it warred with the
evidence—the attack, the spikes that had killed them, Raechelle's crimson eyes. Every instinct screamed that this
couldn't be true, but I'd seen too much to deny it anymore.

The third time through, grief overwhelmed everything else. This was Mom's last message to me. These were some of the final
words she'd taken the time to write down, knowing—knowing—that she might not survive to speak them herself. She'd been
preparing for this, trying to protect me even in death. "Our love and guidance will always be with you." But I wanted her
here, not just her love. I wanted to ask her questions, to hear her voice one more time, to tell her I wasn't ready for
any of this.

By the fourth reading, I started to notice the smaller details. The movie references—Mary Jane, Gamora, Belle,
Rapunzel—chosen specifically because she knew they would resonate with me. The way she called Raechelle "Rae," suggesting
an intimacy, a friendship I'd never known existed. The fact that Raechelle had been watching over me since childhood,
that we'd met before and some spell had made us both forget. My entire life had been carefully orchestrated without my
knowledge.

And then, at the very end: "With Great Power Comes Great Responsibility! Hahaha, see what I did there?"

A laugh burst out of me—loud, unexpected, edged with hysteria. It probably echoed off the walls like the cackle of a
madman. She'd quoted Spider-Man. In her final letter, explaining that I was some kind of supernatural heir with enormous
magical abilities, entrusting me to a vampire familiar I was apparently mystically bonded to, warning me about enemies and
destinies... she'd made a Spider-Man reference. Because she knew it would make me laugh. Because even facing death, even
preparing her son for an impossible future, she was still Mom.

"She quoted Spider-Man, of all things," I muttered to myself, a bittersweet smile tugging at my lips as I wiped away the
tears that had unexpectedly spilled from my eyes—when had I started crying? The absurdity of it all—a superhero quote
slipping into such a surreal moment—felt strangely comforting. It was so perfectly her that for a moment, I could almost
hear her laughing with me.

But the laughter faded quickly, leaving behind only the ache of loss. She was gone. Dad was gone. And I was here, holding
this letter, expected to somehow become what they'd believed I could be.
In the corner of the bedroom, Raechelle stood silently, her presence blending with the night as though they were one.
She watched me with concern etched on her face before she finally spoke up. “I am deeply sorry for the loss of your
parents,” she said, her voice soft yet steady. “They have always been incredibly kind to me, and I will miss them
greatly. They were some of the only people I could truly call friends.”

Her words wrapped around me like a warm embrace, and I could sense her genuine sorrow. There was a moment of silence
between us, heavy with shared grief and unsaid thoughts. Then she continued, her eyes searching mine, “Did your mother
mention anything about me? What else did she mention? If you dont mind me asking?”

I noted the subtle hint of sadness that laced her voice, a melancholic echo that seemed to linger in the air between us.
Her crimson eyes, bright yet edged with curiosity, scanned my face, searching for something unspoken. Despite the late
hour, the room was dimly lit, with only the pale moonlight filtering through the window, casting soft shadows that
danced across her form. The darkness around her seemed to cloak her further, her features obscured by the oversized,
baggy clothes that hung loosely from her frame.

As I studied her, a flurry of thoughts raced through my mind about how best to approach Rachelle. Should I reveal the
profound connection I just learned existed between us? Or would it be wiser to keep that revelation locked away for now,
especially since I still had no clear understanding of her identity? My mother’s words echoed in my mind—she had assured
me that I could trust this girl, but uncertainty gnawed at me.

After a few moments of contemplation, I opted to keep the nature of our bond to myself for the time being, waiting for
the right moment to unveil it. Instead, I chose to share the other, more tangible truths: “She mentioned that you are a
cat familiar, deeply entwined with my bloodline. And that you are, in fact, a child of blood—a vampire.”

As I spoke, a mix of excitement and trepidation surged within me. I was keenly aware of the impact my words might have
on her, uncertain about how she would react to my revelations. To my surprise, she simply nodded, her acknowledgment
serving as a quiet confirmation of what my mother had written. Encouraged by her response, I took a breath and
continued, eager to unravel the mystery that enveloped us both.

“She briefly explained the intricacies of magic, the various Archons, and the reasons behind my ignorance regarding
these matters. Her revelations felt like shards of glass piercing through the fog that had clouded my mind for so long.
Most startling of all, she informed me that I am the Blood Archon — a title that resonated deeply within me, though I
couldn’t fully grasp its weight. She also cautioned me about potential adverse side effects associated with my newfound
identity. As if that weren’t enough, she mentioned a ring, a small, seemingly innocuous artifact, that would shield me
from detection by others.”

After her mention of the ring, my gaze was drawn back into the hidden compartment where it lay nestled in the back
corner, catching the moonlight like a dark star.

I reached in, the cool metal of the ring contrasting against my warm fingers, and upon closer inspection, I realized it
appeared to be nothing more than a simple black band, devoid of embellishments or markings. It seemed impossible that
such an ordinary item could possess the power she described. Driven by a mix of curiosity and urgency, I slipped it onto
my middle finger. To my astonishment, it shrank and molded itself to fit snugly against my skin, a clear sign of the
enchantment woven into its very essence.

Raechelle inhaled sharply at that moment, triggering an instinctual jolt of anxiety within me. I turned to her, startled
by the small gasp that escaped her lips.

“What’s wrong?” I asked, my voice laced with concern.

“I can no longer sense your magic,” she replied, her eyes widening with a mixture of surprise and relief. “That’s a good
sign and will greatly benefit us moving forward. Now that the cat is out of the bag, so to speak.” A smirk danced on her
lips, and she let out a soft chuckle that broke the tension in the air. “But we can’t stay here, at least for a couple
of days, until things settle down. After the attack, there will be those who sensed the sudden surge of energy. We
should find a safe spot in case they come looking for us.”

She pointed toward the shadowy expanse outside. “Fortunately, my home isn’t far from here; in fact, we won’t even need
to leave the property. There, we can rest and regroup. I am utterly exhausted, and we need to have a thorough discussion
about everything that’s happened.”

Without wasting another moment, she urged, “Quickly, gather the journal and anything else you think you’ll need. We must
move swiftly.”

I nodded, urgency coursing through my veins, and started to gather some clothes, essentials, and the journal that held mysteries of its own. Within minutes, we stood at the threshold.

As I reached for the door handle, Raechelle's hand shot out, gripping my wrist. Her fingers were ice-cold.

"Wait." Her voice was barely a whisper, but the fear in it froze me in place. "Do you hear that?"

I strained my ears. At first, nothing. Then—there. A sound so faint I almost thought I imagined it. A rhythmic thumping, like distant footfalls. Multiple sets, getting closer with each passing second.

"They're already here," she breathed, her crimson eyes wide with alarm. "They found us faster than I expected. We need to go. Now."

We burst out of the house into the cool night air. I felt the weight of the world on my shoulders, but also a flicker of something else—not quite hope. More like the desperate determination of prey that refuses to be caught. Raechelle led the way toward the tree line at a near-run, and I followed without hesitation, leaving behind the only home I'd ever known.

Behind us, I could have sworn I heard the front door crash open.
